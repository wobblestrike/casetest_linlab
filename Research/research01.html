Design of Smart Nanogenerators with High Outputs

In the past decades, increasing research efforts have been devoted to renewable energy owing to the largely increased energy consumption. Searching for renewable energy with reduced carbon emissions, secure long-term energy supply, and less dependence on fossil fuel is mandatory for the sustainable development of the world. Nanogenerators, which are emerging new energy technologies that harvest renewable energy from mechanical vibration in the environment, are capable of fulfilling the above-mentioned features and have thus attracted global attention. Since 2006, piezoelectric nanogenerator (PENG) has been developed to efficiently convert tiny-scale mechanical vibration into electricity. Until recently (2012), another cost-effective, easy-fabricating, and robust triboelectric nanogenerator (TENG) has been created based on the contact electrification effect and electrostatic induction. In this research, we want to design the nanogenerators with high outputs and different structures for specific applications. For example, we use BaTiO3 nanotubes and polymer poly(dimethylsiloxane) (PDMS) composite to fabricate a PENG with stretchable and flexible characteristics. The open-circuit voltage and short-circuit current density of the PENG reached a high level of 5.5 V and 350 nA/cm2, respectively. The output can be used to directly drive a commercial liquid crystal display (LCD). Regarding the TENG, the most exciting result is that we successfully demonstrate that the concept of water-solid surface contact electrification can be used to harvest water wave and water drop energy. This new prototype water-TENG provide an open-circuit voltage of 52 V and a short-circuit current density of 2.45 mA/m2 with a peak power density of nearly 0.13 W/m2, which is able to drive 60 LED bulbs simultaneously. Compared with traditional TENGs that are designed for the contact of solid materials, this study opens the possibility in utilizing liquid movements and extends its application scope.
