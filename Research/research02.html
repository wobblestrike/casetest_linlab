Development of Self-powered Nanosensors and Nanosystems

Self-powered nanosensors and nanosystems that can function without external power sources have recently been demonstrated as new approaches for pH, temperature, biomolecules, toxic pollutants, and light sensing. The working principle is based on combining/integrating nanogenerators with sensors. The nanogenerators will harvest energy from the environment to power the sensors. Owing to its convenient monitoring mechanism, the self-powered nanosensors and nanosystems could be the most desirable and promising prototype for environmental protection/detection in the near future because no battery is needed to power the device. For the time being, the major challenge is how to develop fully integrated, stand-alone and self-powered nanosensors and nanosystems. In this research, we first demonstrate the triboelectric effect-based sensor for the detection of Hg2+ ions by using 3-mercaptopropionic acid modified Au nanoparticles as electrical performance enhancer and recognition element. By replacing the Au nanoparticles with TiO2 nanomaterial array, the rationally designed TENG can be sued to detect catechin molecules. A fully integrated and self-powered UV photodetector based on TENG configuration is also fabricated. 3D dendritic TiO2 nanostructures were applied as the built-in photodetector and contact material of the TENG.
