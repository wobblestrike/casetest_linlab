Fabrication of Porous Metal Nanomaterials for Electrochemical Applications

The preparation of inexpensive and efficient electrocatalytic materials for electrochemical applications is currently an active research area. Nanomaterials are more attractive than their bulk counterparts for use as catalytic materials because of their greater surface areas and higher catalytic activities. In electrochemical applications, nanomaterials are commonly dispersed on carbon supports to provide high electrical conductivity and acceptable chemical stability at low cost. Nevertheless, carbon-supported nanocatalysts usually suffer from problems associated with poor loading, low dispersion, easy exfoliation, and aggregation. To overcome the problems, we first prepare several porous Pt nanomaterials (nanonetworks, nanosponges, and nanodendrites) with high surface areas and great number of absorption for electrocatalytic reaction. Bimetallic Pt/Au nanomaterials are also fabricated in order to further enhance the electrocatalytic activities. In addition, we also try to discover non-metal nanomaterials for electrochemical applications because they are more cost-effective. For example, we find that Te nanowire can be used for selective (as compared to ascorbic acid and uric acid) and sensitive detection of dopamine (LOD of 1 nM).
